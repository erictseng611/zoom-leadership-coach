## 5. Areas for Growth

### 1. Proactive Time Management - Meeting Boundaries

**What Happened:**
The meeting ran 15 minutes over schedule. This isn't just about efficiency—it signals to the team that scheduled time commitments are flexible.

**Why It Matters:**
- You have "respect others' time" as a stated value
- Emma had to leave at the scheduled end time and missed the final action item discussion
- Running over creates a cascade effect on everyone's schedules

**Specific Action:**
- Set a timer for 5 minutes before the scheduled end
- When it goes off, do a time check: "We have 5 minutes left. Let's wrap up the key decisions and table the rest."
- Alternative: Designate someone as timekeeper (rotating role for team development)

### 2. Check for Understanding - Don't Leave People Behind

**What Happened:**
During the infrastructure discussion (23:00-28:00), you and Marcus dove deep into Kubernetes architecture. The non-technical participants (Sarah, Jordan from Product) went quiet.

**Why It Matters:**
- Your leadership principle emphasizes "build psychological safety"
- When people can't follow the discussion, they disengage
- Sarah and Jordan need to understand the constraints to make good product decisions

**Specific Action:**
- When going technical, pause periodically and ask: "Does this make sense? Should I explain this differently?"
- Better yet: Ask Marcus to summarize the key constraint in non-technical terms
- This respects different expertise levels and models that you value all perspectives

### 3. Document Decision Context - Help Future You

**What Happened:**
You made a clear decision to defer PWA work to Q3, but didn't capture *why* in the meeting notes.

**Why It Matters:**
- In 3 months, someone will ask "Why did we push this?" and you'll be fuzzy on the specifics
- The team needs to understand decision rationale to make aligned choices
- This is part of your "communicate reasoning" principle

**Specific Action:**
- When making a significant decision, take 30 seconds to state: "Let me capture the why behind this decision..."
- Include: What data informed it, what tradeoffs you considered, what constraint was most important
- Add this to your action item for documenting the architecture decision
